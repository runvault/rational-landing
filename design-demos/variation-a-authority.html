The Crisis

Accounting is running out of people.

340,000 accountants and auditors have left the profession since 2019. Not retired — quit. They moved to tech, to consulting, to anything that didn't mean another month-end close at 11 PM.

The pipeline is broken. CPA exam candidates are down 33% in a decade. Universities are closing accounting programs. The people who understand debits and credits are disappearing, and they're not coming back.

Meanwhile, every open role costs $70–95K fully loaded — if you can fill it at all. The average time-to-hire for a senior accountant is now 62 days. That's two months of backlogs, missed close deadlines, and your best people burning out covering the gap.
