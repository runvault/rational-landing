The Crisis

Accounting is running out of people.

340,000 accountants and auditors have left the profession since 2019. They're not coming back. The pipeline is broken — CPA exam candidates dropped 33% in five years. Firms are turning away work because there's no one to do it.

The pipeline is structurally broken. Universities are producing fewer graduates. The 150-credit-hour requirement deters talent. Junior hires who do start leave within 3 years for tech and finance roles that pay more and demand less.

Meanwhile, the loaded cost of a mid-level staff accountant — salary, benefits, training, turnover — runs $70–95K per year. And you still face 3-month ramp-up times, annual attrition, and knowledge loss every time someone walks out the door.
