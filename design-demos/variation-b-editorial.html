The Crisis

Accounting is running out of people.

Since 2019, over 340,000 accountants and auditors have left the profession. They didn't move to other firms. They left entirely — driven out by grinding hours, repetitive tasks, and a profession that hasn't changed how it works in decades.

The pipeline is broken at both ends. College enrollment in accounting programs has dropped 17% in five years. The students who do graduate increasingly choose consulting, tech, or finance over a career reconciling intercompany transactions. The average CPA is 54 years old. Retirement is coming faster than replacement.

Meanwhile, the work keeps growing. Every company needs books closed, invoices coded, expenses matched. The loaded cost of a mid-level accountant is $70,000 to $95,000 — when you can find one at all. For growing companies, this isn't a talent shortage. It's an existential bottleneck.
